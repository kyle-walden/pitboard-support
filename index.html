FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS (FAQ)


What does Pitboard do?

Track your lap times, analyze corner and straight performance, and improve your riding with detailed metrics. Use session history to pinpoint areas for improvement.

Do I need special equipment to use the app?

A sturdy phone mount is essential. We recommend an anti-shake alloy or metal handlebar holder. Mounting on yourself may reduce data accuracy.

Can I place my phone on myself instead of the bike?

The phone needs to calibrate to the bike's lean axis, so mounting on the handlebars is best. Using a moon bag or tripper may cause inaccurate data.

What metrics are tracked?
  • Total ride time
  • Number of laps
  • Current lap time
  • Corner time
  • Straight time
  • Average lap time
  • Average corner and straight times
How do I use Pitboard's data to get faster?

Try to keep your current lap times at or below your average lap times to improve performance. Use the session history to monitor progress.

Does the app require internet?

No, it uses your phone’s GPS and sensors. No internet connection is needed during sessions.

How accurate is GPS tracking?

Accuracy depends on signal strength and device quality. Ensure a clear sky view and wait for GPS lock before starting.

Why aren't my laps logging?

Poor GPS signal can affect lap detection. Make sure you have a strong GPS signal (green icon) and wait for GPS lock.

Can I review previous sessions?

Yes, all sessions are stored locally for review in the Session History tab.

What happens if I fall mid-ride?

The timers keep running. Fall detection is planned for future updates.

Can I export or share my data?

This feature is coming soon.

Can I name my sessions?

Yes, you can rename sessions after recording for easy identification.

Does the app work on all tracks?

Yes, on any outdoor lap-based track with GPS coverage.

How is a new lap detected?

The app uses your start position and detects when you cross a 10-meter radius around it to log laps.

Can I use the app for enduro or trail riding?

Yes, the app works best on closed-loop, lap-based tracks.

Why is the app prematurely logging laps?

Your track may be too tight. The lap detection radius is 10 meters; tight corners can cause early lap logs.

WHAT WE DO WITH YOUR INFO

Privacy Policy

Pitboard respects your privacy. The app does not collect, store, or share any personal data beyond what is necessary for its core functionality.

GPS Permission: The app requests access to your device’s GPS solely to track your location during rides for lap timing and performance metrics. This data is processed locally on your device and is never transmitted or stored externally.

No internet connection is required for the app to function, and no personal data leaves your device unless you choose to share it manually.
